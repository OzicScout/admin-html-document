First, lets go over ledger.


Ledger is our new replacement for Co-Inspect. As we are on fabric, without plugins, this was our best choice. Now yes, it different than Co-Inspect, however, if you can get quite good with it, its actually more useful!
Ledger is started by using "/ledger" in-game. After that you will find a range of commands, that you may see are similar to Co-Inspect, just worded differently. These commands consist of:

    - /ledger i (which is the same as /ledger inspect)
    - /legder search - /ledger rollback - /ledger restore

Now, there are some more legder commands in game, however, they are unimportant to us
In this document I will cover how to use the important ones listed
Lets first do: /ledger i
/ledger i, (also know as /legder inspect) is the same as /co-inspect on the last/first SMP!
All you have to do is: enable /ledger i in game, then, punch (left click) your desired block/entity and you will be able see all logs you need to sort an issue!

Next let's go over: /ledger search
/ledger search is the legder equivalent of /co lookup from the last/first SMP!
You can search items, blocks, actions, players, etc.
To see if a player, for say, stole and item, you would use the command:
/ledger search source:[player name] action:[item-remove] after:[after an amount of time, if you want to search a large amount, I suggest 2weeks] range:[amout of blocks, or if you want global, @global] This would show you where, and when they stole/or removed a specific item, [if you use: object:specific item wanted] after the action.
Or if you just want to see what a player has done in a specific amount of time, but without a specific action, you can just use:
/ledger search source:[player name] after:[amount of time]

Next up, /ledger rollback
As suggested in the name, /legder rollback is the same as /co rollback from the last/1st SMP!
Let's say you wanted to rollback a stealer, you would suggestively use the following command:
/ledger rollback source:[player name] action:[item-remove] after:[amount of time] range:[amount of blocks, or if you want global, @global
You can customise these values to your situation, much like you can in /ledger search

And finally, /ledger restore
/legder restore is the same as /co undo from the last/1st SMP, howvever it is mainly for rollbacks. It undos a rollback that you did.
Lets say that you falsely rollback somebody, and needed to undo that, you would:
b>/ledger restore source:[player name] action:[item-remove] after:[amount of time] range:[amount of blocks, or if you want global, @global
As said in the last 2 commands, you can customise these values to your situation. The values should match whatever rollback you're undoing.
